The History of the Chicken

From Apex Predator to Professional Egg-Layer.

1. The "Don't Eat Me" Era (68 Million Years Ago)

Before they were nuggets, chickens were basically T-Rexes . Science shows they are the closest living relative to the king of dinosaurs . Imagine a T-Rex, but instead of roaring, it just wants a handful of corn.

2. The Jungle Fever (8,000 Years Ago)

3. The Roman Resort (800 BC)

The Romans went wild for chickens. They invented the omelet and even tried to fatten birds with bread soaked in wine . One Roman law in 161 BC actually tried to limit people to eating only one chicken per meal to stop "moral decay" .

4. Modern Day Superstars

Today, chickens outnumber humans 3-to-1 . They can recognize 100 different faces and even have their own "language" with over 30 different sounds .

Mind-Blowing Fact: The longest recorded flight of a chicken is only 13 seconds . They aren't lazy; they're just "ground-based enthusiasts."
